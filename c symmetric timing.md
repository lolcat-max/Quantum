## C-Symmetric Atomic Timing

In this model, the "c-symmetric timing" of an atom refers to the fundamental role of charge conjugation in determining the temporal phase of its wavefunction. Because CPT symmetry (Charge-Parity-Time) is a conserved law, any violation or oscillation in the charge state of a superposed particle must be mirrored by a corresponding shift in its temporal coordinate.[^7][^2][^8][^9]

- **Phase Coordination**: The C-symmetry acts as a "clock" that ensures the internal state of an atom remains synchronized with the manifold's background geometry.[^2][^4]
- **Atomic Coordinates**: Superposition coordinates are not static points but vibrational displacements that oscillate around a mean position, guided by this temporal symmetry.[^10]
- **State Symmetrization**: Identical particles produced in spacelike-separated events undergo a dynamical "timing" process that enforces global state symmetry across the manifold.[^11]


## Black Holes as Uncoordinated Voids

Black holes are traditionally viewed as dense objects, but current research suggests they are better understood as "spacetime geometries" that lack standard coordinate coordination. These "uncoordinated voids" represent regions where the symmetry groups (such as $C(s)$ or $C(2)$) of the vacuum are broken, creating a topological hole in the manifold's timing structure.[^12][^3][^13]
